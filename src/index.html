Sendspin is an open standard for the complete music listening experience: listen, control & see.

Sendspin is a work in progress. The specification is subject to change.

Sendspin is an open standard by the Open Home Foundation for a synchronized music experience across multiple devices and rooms. It enables speakers, lights, and screens to work together to create a rich and immersive audio environment:

  • Play audio in sync across multiple speakers
  • Visualize audio playback on connected lights
  • Offer music control and metadata from tablets or screens

Try it out today

Sendspin is actively being developed, and we have working implementations that we're using to test the specification and validate the experience we want to deliver.

You can try a development version of Sendspin today:

  • Stream music via Sendspin to any client using the beta version of Music Assistant and play the stream simultaneously in browsers and Google Cast-enabeled devices1.
  • Play a Sendspin stream on the Home Assistant Voice Preview Edition (or a connected speaker via the AUX port) running a special Sendspin build
  • Show album art and control your music from ESPHome using this example configuration
  • Play a Sendspin stream from the command-line client using sendspin-cli

If you have any feedback, please stop by at the #sendspin-beta-testing channel on the Music Assistant Discord.

1 Enable experimental Sendspin mode in the settings of each Cast player in Music Assistant.

Roles in a Sendspin network

Sendspin supports different device roles to create a complete listening experience:

  • Speaker – Any device that plays audio (speaker, phone etc.)
  • Visualizer – Devices that visualize playback, such as LED strips, TouchDesigner plugin or a Winamp-style visualizers
  • Remote – Devices that display metadata and allow the user to control the music

Why (yet another) new protocol?

Major audio-playback standards like AirPlay and Cast are closed.

We believe users should control their devices, their data, and their music. Open standards are essential to that vision. Existing protocols for synchronized audio experiences are proprietary and closed. This makes it impossible to have an open ecosystem of interoperable solutions that unleashes the creativity of the community.

Sendspin is an open standard that takes a unique approach by focusing on the complete music listening experience. It goes beyond support for multi-room audio playback and supports remote controls and audio visualization out of the box.
